What is Diversity and Inclusion?

Diversity

Diversity refers to the presence of differences within a given setting. In the workplace, it encompasses various characteristics that make individuals unique.

A diverse workforce is one that includes people from different walks of life, with varied perspectives and experiences.

Types of Diversity

There are many characteristics that contribute to diversity; inluding but not limited to:

  • Age
  • Gender Identity
  • Race
  • Sexual Orientation
  • Cultural Background
  • Physical Abilities

Inclusion

Inclusion is about creating an environment where all individuals feel respected, valued and supported.

It ensures that everyone, regardless of their background or identity, has equal access to opportunities, resources, and decision-making processes. While diversity focuses on who is in the workplace, inclusion is about how those individuals are integrated, empowered, and allowed to thrive.

Why be Inclusive?

It Benefits Employers

Diversity and Inclusion is also proven to:

  • Boost Innovation and Creativity
  • Improve Employee Retention and Engagement
  • Enhance Company Reputation and Attracts Talent
  • Increase Financial Performance

So it's clear to see that an inclusive work environment isn't just a moral or ethical choice; it has concrete benefits for businesses.

It Benefits Employees

Prospective employees care about inclusion.

Businesses and organisations with strong diversity and inclusion policies are more attractive to prospective employees, so fostering a diverse and inclusive environment in your workplace will certainly benefit you and your business.

Surveys & Statistics

  • Research from McKinsey shows that companies in the top quartile for gender diversity on executive teams were 25% more likely to have above-average profitability than those in the bottom quartile.
  • A report by Deloitte found that inclusive companies are 1.8 times more likely to be innovation leaders in their industry.
  • The Chartered Institute of Personnel and Development (CIPD) reported that highly inclusive workplaces see 28% lower turnover rates, helping employers save on recruitment and training costs.

How to be Inclusive

Hiring

  • Write inclusive job descriptions by avoiding biased language
  • Advertise roles widely to reach different communities e.g. on LGBTQIA+ job boards
  • Use blind recruitment to avoid recruiting biases
